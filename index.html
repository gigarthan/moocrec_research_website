What is MOOCRec Humanity?

Massive Open Online Courses (MOOCs) have become an important phenomenon in the e-learning space. Availability of platforms like edX, Coursera, Udacity, Futurelearn have made accessing quality content effortless. But in the same time availability of multiple platforms with similar courses poses a challenge for the users to select the right courses for themselves. MoocRec Humanity tries to solve this issue by filtering out the right courses in different platforms based on the user’s learning style. MoocRec considers course videos’ production styles and course practical workloads like quizzes and reading materials to categorize courses with learning styles. Additionally, users can do an advanced querying to search through different MOOCS and filter courses based on the lecturer's accents. styles.

Videos are one of the most important visual mediums which has seen a tremendous growth in volume in the past decade. Growth of giants like YouTube, Hulu, Netflix etc. have made video a popular medium. Abundance of videos in the internet has encouraged in growth of various algorithms which tries to analyze the context and styles of the videos. Recent developments in the field of Machine Learning has enabled researchers and scientists to do large scale computations. Especially breakthroughs in deep learning and introduction of Convolutional Neural Network has given the possibility of identifying images and videos with high accuracy in a limited amount of time. Video classification approaches can be divided into four main groups: text-based approaches, audio-based approaches, visual-based approaches, and those that used some combination of text, audio and visual features . The standard approach to video classification involves three major stages: First, local visual features that describe a region of the video are extracted either densely or at a sparse set of interest points. Next, the features get combined into a fixed-sized level of video description. One of the popular approaches is to quantize all features using a learned k-means dictionary and accumulate the visual words over the duration of the video into histograms of varying spatio - temporal positions and extents. Lastly, a classifier (such as SVM) is trained on the resulting bag of words representation to distinguish among the visual classes of interest . Furthermore, combination of Recurrent Neural Network and LSTM technique is experimented which can deal with complexity, ambiguity, uncertainty and easily target the situations where complex service behavior can be deviated from user’s expectation . MOOC video classification based on learner style and presentation style has been experimented in. Comparing with MoocRec, the system can identify the learning style for courses falling under technical category and the learning model which has been used is FSLSM. Research Problem

Currently, the passion towards learning new subject areas among the community is noticeably increased. Due to technological advancement e-learning has provided proper assistance for the above-mentioned community who are keen on learning new subject areas. Under the e-learning MOOCs are the most popular relatively reason learning tendency with various platforms available like Coursera, Edx , Udacity, Futurelearn etc. The openness of the MOOC platforms is one of the major reasons of its popularity among huge audience coming from different backgrounds all over the world. With the popularity of this particular concept, more and new MOOC providers step in to the field where the different displaying methods of information are available. This brings much inconvenience to the users when choosing the best option. As recent researches reveal, the statistics of the learners who drop the e-learning courses before completion become highly increasing. The major reasons for dropping courses as revealed are the discrepancy among the personal requirement and the course content such as presentation styles, teaching styles and time etc. Under such a situation, the importance of one unified platform where the learners can choose courses based on their preferences is highly demandable. The personalization of learning styles is a big talking point over the recent years to provide learning materials according to the preferences of learners. In the meantime, it would be much more supportive, considering the accent and gender of the tutor. AS researches revealed, the language is one of the key major factors to master new subject. As several studies quoted, the leaning or mastering new subject become more effortless when the resources are provided appropriately. Currently available MOOCs search engines do not provide the facility to search courses depending on the learning styles associated with gender and the accent of the tutor. Taking the above-mentioned facts into consideration, the existing huge demand of a unified platform for recommending MOOCs depending on learner style of the user and the search engine which allows the users to filter more advanced multiple fields can be concluded.

Objectives

The proposed system is a research which is lying over the following general objectives.

  • MOOCs search engine with search between edX, Coursera and Futurelearn with more accurate information and relevant to the user learner style
  • Recommend the most appropriate courses based on learners’ learning style and preferences such as accent and gender of the tutor.
  • Develop user friendly interfaces with the purpose of convenient interaction with the system.
  • Help users identify their learning style based on VARK model.
  • Explore and understand the application of deep learning techniques in various scenarios.

Specific Objectives

  • Maintain the user profile considering user’s learning style and domain knowledge.
  • Optimize MOOCs search engine, search between all the course modules contexts. (video transcripts pdfs, text tutorials) with given any keyword/s or topic/s.
  • Classify course video styles and find the average video styles used in the course
  • Classify the audio of videos based on the English talking accent and gender of the tutor.
  • Identify and classify different type of learning materials in a MOOC course.
  • Quantify each learning material in a specific MOOC course and recommend the best to the learner.
